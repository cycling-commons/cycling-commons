Build the Commons with us.

The Cycling Commons is built by the people who use it — in code, in local knowledge, and in everything around them. It's early, it's open, and there's room for you.

A commons doesn't grow by hiring; it grows by people showing up. Here's where we need hands.

The heart of it

Riders & local knowledge.

The Commons is only as good as what riders know: the climb worth the detour, where the water is, the junction that bites, the loop that's quietly perfect. That knowledge is the whole point — and it's the one thing no one can build for you.

Honest note — the contribution system isn't live yet. What you're on is a working prototype: the flows are real, but nothing saves. Here's how you can seed the Commons today, no waiting required:
  • Map objective facts in OpenStreetMap. Water points, repair stations, surfaces — add them to OpenStreetMap now. We build on OSM and contribute back, so it flows into the Commons. Nothing here needed.
  • Shape the prototype. Click through the map, add a climb, or a voting round, then email us — or open an issue — where it doesn't match how you'd actually enter your data. Right now, feedback is the contribution.
  • Send local knowledge. Contribute a place on GitHub — a climb, a hazard, a great ride — and we'll curate it as the Commons fills.
  • Be first in. Leave your email and we'll tell you the moment open contribution goes live.
