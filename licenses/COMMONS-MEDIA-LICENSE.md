# Cycling Commons — Media Licence (CC BY-SA 4.0)

> text (see "Full text" below).

## The licence
**Media** contributed to the Cycling Commons — photographs, video, and similar creative works
(for example a photo of a climb, a viewpoint, or a repair station) — is made available under the
**Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 4.0 International licence (CC BY-SA 4.0)**.

Anyone may **share and adapt** the media, including commercially, provided they:
1. **Attribute** the creator (and the Cycling Commons); and
2. **Share alike** — distribute adaptations under CC BY-SA 4.0.

Why a different licence from the data: ODbL is built for *databases*; CC BY-SA is built for
*creative works*. The data uses ODbL, media uses CC BY-SA — both open, both share-alike, and
intentionally aligned in spirit.

## Required attribution
> Photo / media by **[creator]**, via the Cycling Commons, licensed under CC BY-SA 4.0.

## Provenance — the hard rule
Media may enter the Commons **only** if the contributor owns it or has the right to license it.
- Your own photos / video: yes.
- Someone else's copyrighted images, scraped or re-hosted from another site or a closed
  database: **no.**
By contributing media you confirm you hold these rights (see the Terms clause). This is what keeps
the open Commons free of infringement — never scrape, never re-host third-party media.

## Scope
Covers contributed media only. It does **not** cover personal data (private, never published),
the underlying factual data (ODbL — see `COMMONS-DATA-LICENSE.md`), or any proprietary software and
branding built on the Commons.
