## X. The Open Commons

**X.1 What the Commons is.** The "Commons" is the database of *non-personal* cycling information
contributed by users and the community — for example climbs, points of interest, bike-friendly
stays, road conditions, and area and road geometry. It does **not** include your personal data
(your identity, history, fitness metrics, or movements), which is private and
processed in accordance with the GDPR and our Privacy Policy.

**X.2 How we license the Commons (outbound).** We publish the Commons openly so that anyone —
including commercial users — may use, share, and build on it for free:
- **Data** is licensed under the **Open Database License v1.0 (ODbL)** (contents under DbCL 1.0).
- **Media** (photos, video) is licensed under **Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 4.0
  International (CC BY-SA 4.0)**.

Use is subject to the conditions of those licences (attribution, share-alike, keep-open).

**X.3 Your contribution (inbound grant).** When you contribute non-personal data or media to the
Commons, you grant the Commons steward (BikeCoders, or the independent foundation that succeeds it)
and the public a **perpetual, irrevocable, worldwide, royalty-free licence** to use, reproduce,
adapt, publish, and sublicense that contribution as part of the Commons under the licences in X.2.
You keep ownership of your own media; this grant simply lets us — and everyone — keep it open.

**X.4 Your warranty.** You confirm that you have the right to contribute what you submit and that
it infringes no one's rights. Contribute facts you observed and media you created or are licensed
to share — **never** data or images copied, scraped, or re-hosted from a third party (for example
a closed climb database or another platform).

**X.5 What is not in the Commons.** Your personal and private data (X.1) is never published as part
of the Commons. Third-party or partner content (for example a closed accommodation scheme's exact
host addresses) is not part of the Commons; where we surface such options we link out to the
provider rather than republishing their data.

**X.6 Our commitment.** We will keep the Commons under open, share-alike licences. We will not
re-close it or place it behind a paywall. The Commons is intended to be handed to an independent
foundation (a Dutch *Stichting*) that holds it in trust for the public (see Governance).

**X.7 Privacy & GDPR.** We adhere to the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR): we collect
only the personal data we need, process it lawfully and only as described in our Privacy Policy, and
never publish it as part of the Commons. You can exercise your GDPR rights — access, rectification,
erasure, and the rest — by contacting info@cyclingcommons.org.
