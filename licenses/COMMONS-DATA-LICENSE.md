## The licence
The **Cycling Commons** — the database of *non-personal* cycling data (climbs, points of interest,
bike-friendly stays, road conditions, area and road geometry, and similar; see
`../wiki/data-catalog.md`) — is made available under the
**Open Database License v1.0 (ODbL)**. Where it applies, the individual *contents* of the
database are made available under the **Database Contents License v1.0 (DbCL)**. This mirrors
OpenStreetMap.

Anyone — including commercial users — may **share, create, and adapt**
the Commons, for free, provided they:
1. **Attribute** it (see below);
2. **Share alike** — publish any adapted database under ODbL; and
3. **Keep it open** — do not use technical measures (e.g. DRM) to restrict access; if a
   "Produced Work" is distributed with such measures, also distribute a version without them.

## Scope — what this covers, and what it does NOT
- **Covers:** the non-personal, community-contributed Commons data.
- **Does NOT cover:**
  - **Personal data** — a person's identity, history, fitness metrics, and movements.
    Never published as part of the Commons; processed only in accordance with the **GDPR** and
    our Privacy Policy (see Manifesto §IV).
  - **Media** (photos, video) — licensed separately under CC BY-SA 4.0 (see `COMMONS-MEDIA-LICENSE.md`).
  - **Third-party / partner data** — e.g. a closed accommodation scheme's exact host addresses.
    Signposted via referral, never absorbed into the Commons.
  - **The platform code** — source-available under the **PolyForm Shield License 1.0.0**
    (see [`../LICENSE`](../LICENSE)); a separate licence from the Commons data.
  - **Proprietary branding or game experiences built on the Commons** — proprietary; not part of
    the Commons.

## Privacy (GDPR)
The Cycling Commons adheres to the EU **General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)**. The Commons
itself holds only non-personal data. Any personal data is minimised, processed lawfully and only as
described in our Privacy Policy, and is **never** published as part of the Commons. GDPR requests or
questions: info@cyclingcommons.org.
